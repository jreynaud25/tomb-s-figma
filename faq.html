What is a Tomb?

The Tombs are part of a series of drawings of various hard-format data storage media, originally created in 2016 as part of David Rudnick’s Yale MFA thesis project, Atlas. Each Tomb in the series is drawn using the same tools as the originals; Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator CS3, and are drawn entirely using the trackpad of a 2012 Apple Macbook Pro. No 3D software or additional hardware has been used in their construction. Though some Tombs share common components, each is individually and entirely composed, drawn and titled for this series. There are no generative components.


What is a House?

The 177 Tombs are divided into 8 Houses. Each House has its own themes, launch mechanics, on-chain behaviours, restrictions, evolutions, and printed and physical components. The specific aspects of the houses and their behaviours are detailed in the Tomb Index.

Why 177 Tombs?

I wanted a unique, identifiable number large enough for the possibilities of interaction and composition to feel infinite, small enough for every one to matter and for its unique qualities to feel special, and for me to be capable of drawing them all to a standard where no Tomb felt less interesting or compelling than any other.

Whats a RONIN? Who are the collaborators?

RONIN are the Tombs of the eighth and final House. Each of the 16 RONIN are collaborations, and recognitions of past collaborations, with some of the people whose work has helped bring my practice to this point, or whose works on chain have helped inspire and establish the benchmarks and paths that this project continues to explore. Each RONIN Tomb is drawn by me but launches as a passenger in a contract deployed by the collaborator. They are diplomats inside embassies, relics inside other territories, distributed language that leaves any contract space this project defines and takes the Tomb Series out into territories defined by other communities, other agencies, other protocols and parameters, non-native to its own logics and goals.

What’s in the Index?

Tomb Index is the definitive catalogue and guide to the Tomb Series. Designed in Terrain, it will be printed with a unique print profile developed specifically for the book, which has involved an 8 colour calibration process. It contains large-format reproductions of all 177 Tombs, detailed breakdowns of each of their elements, plus additional appendices documenting all 177 Disc Surfaces, plus the 19 additional Shield States of the X2 house, and the 19 Shields. It also contains detailed breakdowns of the mechanics and constitution of all eight Houses in the series, an introductory text by David Rudnick, and three special introductory texts by Christopher S. Wood from Forgery, Replica, Fiction, the original text of which is currently out of print, as well as information and details of the series that will only ever be published in the Index itself.
